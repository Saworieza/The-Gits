                                Ian Shere • 6 months ago

                                Demongo - yes I agree. Personally, I found only a few of these that I wouldn't have immediately clicked the back button. I try to build sites that have navigation-for-dumbies as stock standard. Why do designers call this sort of thing "edgy" or "pushing the envelope"? Surely good design (whether on the web or architecturally or graphically) is about user experience. Many of these make me think of one of the webs most horrible experiments - mystery meat navigation.

                                AND, I too, wonder what Google will think of one page sites. We've long been told more pages = authority (yes, I admit spammy/affiliate sites that are one page do rank well), but maybe we've been sold a line there. For me, I refuse to do 1 page sites. Again, user experience. Even if Google is OK with them, I would judge a company and it's one page site to be less than a company with a 10, or 100, page site. Rightly or wrongly, it's about appearance.
